Concluding Remarks

Key takeaways from Finnish unemployment law and recent cases.

Broad Definition

"Entrepreneur" is a broad concept in unemployment law. You do not need official registration or even profit to be considered one.

Strict Restrictions

Allowed activities are narrow. Be very cautious with unpaid work at companies; it is generally not permitted and risks benefit suspension.

Burden of Proof

Part-time entrepreneurship is possible, but proving you are available for full-time work is difficult. Evidence (e.g., concurrent work) is key.

Case-by-Case

Decisions rely on holistic assessment: intent, remuneration, time spent, and organization type all matter in determining "work."

Practical Recommendation

Always document your hours and income strictly. Consult KEHA-Centres, Kela, or your unemployment fund early before starting new activities to avoid unpleasant surprises.
